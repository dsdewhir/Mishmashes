Your files are your life.
Your grant proposal. Next year's marketing budget. The irreplacable pictures of your children. Your life is digital, written in files large and small -- and a virus, a lightning strike, a failed hard drive, or any of countless other mishaps can lose it all forever. But your life is also digital -- and you need access to it from anywhere.
Would you put something precious on a cloud?
Of course not. A cloud is insubstantial -- and it's a long drop to the ground. The same is true of cloud computing: Servers crash. Servers are hacked. Cloud storage companies go out of business. Ask yourself one more question: Is that really the kind of environment to which you're going to entrust your entire digital life?

Be safe and secure. Your own files on your own computer -- anywhere.
